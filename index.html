How to
Pair Program

Why this talk?

  1. Make pairing sessions more productive

    by sharing some tips

  2. Prevent discouragement while pairing

    by highlighting some challenges

  3. Promote pairing

    to those who have never done it before

Why Me?

  1. Lots of pairing for last five years

  2. New team about every three to nine months

  3. Paired with a lot of different personalities

My first pairing session

  1. Nothing accomplished

  2. Constant arguing

  3. Completely exhausted

  4. Skeptical

Rules of the Road

  1. Two Drivers

  2. Two Keyboards (or laptop + keyboard)

  3. Two Monitors

    Mirror displays, Mac OS remembers settings

Share the Road

So share the workstation

Rules of the Road (continued)

  1. No Twitter

  2. No email

  3. Absolutely no Facebook

  4. Important phone calls OK

  5. Communicate other obligations

Have a specific, agreed-upon task

Think outloud

Keep your pair alert, engaged, and informed

Need some "standard" dev environment

  1. Can't expect your pair to learn all your shortcuts

    Give them up if they interfere

  2. Not everybody wants to learn Vim

    Have something with non-vim bindings ready

  3. Be accommodating to your pair

    Benefits compound over time

What to do when ...

You don't get any space?

Just ask for room (verbal communication)

Practice encroachment, loudly (physical communication)

What to do when ...

You're lost?

Just ask questions

Stop frequently and recap

Talk, talk, talk

What to do when ...

It's been 2 hours and you've accomplished nothing?

Stop refactoring!

Stop weighing every little thing

Get stuff working

Some Thoughts on Remote Pairing

  1. Yes, it can work

  2. Good screen-sharing is a must

  3. Good audio is a must

  4. Good internet is a must

    If you're not up and running in less than 60 seconds, just stop

  5. Prior physical pairing with your pair is a plus

What to Remember

  1. Be accommodating and welcoming

  2. Be open, humble, trust your pair

  3. Communicate: Talk, Talk, Talk

Other Tips

  1. Do not pair on trivial things

    i.e. Add radio button to some page

  2. For research or exploratory work, keep extra laptop handy

  3. Periodic breaks, at your discretion

    Especially when shaving yaks

    Don't disappear for 30 minutes without word of life

More Tips

  1. Think outloud - ALL THE TIME

  2. Mutual agreement on a SMALL goal

    ... before you start

  3. Retrospect frequently

    make sure you're on track

When is it helpful?

  1. Onboarding

  2. Experimenting with new technology

    Extra laptop might be helpful

  3. Non-trivial task
